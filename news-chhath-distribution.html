Chhath Festival Distribution Program

Supporting low-income families with sarees and essential pujan samagri for the Chhath Mahaparva.

Kalikamai Youth Society (KYS) celebrated the spirit of Chhath Mahaparva by organizing a large-scale distribution program targeting underprivileged families in the Kalikamai Rural Municipality (RM). Recognizing that the festival is a time of extreme significance and devotion, KYS ensured that financial constraints did not hinder the celebration for those in need.

Distribution of Sarees and Festival Materials

During the event, our dedicated volunteers coordinated the distribution of new sarees and pujan samagri (festival materials) to over 200 women from marginalized backgrounds. This initiative aimed to preserve the cultural dignity of the community while providing tangible support during the most significant festival of our region.

The distribution took place at the local ward office, where members of our executive committee joined local leaders to personally hand over the materials. By fostering a sense of inclusivity and social solidarity, KYS continues to stand alongside our community during critical cultural milestones.

We remain committed to our mission that no one in Kalikamai should feel left behind, especially during times of communal prayer and celebration. Our "little step" towards cultural support is part of a larger journey toward a more equitable and caring society.
