Helping Poor People During Marriage

Supporting underprivileged families by eliminating financial burdens during weddings and promoting the Anti-Dowry campaign.

The Kalikamai Youth Society (KYS) strongly stands against social evils like the dowry system that heavily burden underprivileged families. As part of our ongoing community welfare initiatives, we have taken an active role in Helping Poor People During Marriage. Recognizing the extreme financial strain a wedding can cause for impoverished parents, KYS stepped in to organize and support these events with dignity and joy.

Key Impacts of the Initiative

  • Financial Relief: We provided essential wedding materials, clothing, and basic household items to the newlyweds, ensuring the bride's family did not have to take on crippling debt.
  • Anti-Dowry Advocacy: The weddings were conducted strictly without dowry, setting a powerful precedent in the community and breaking the cycle of financial extortion disguised as tradition.
  • Community Solidarity: By organizing these marriages collectively, we fostered a sense of unity where the entire village came forward to bless the couples rather than judging them on their socio-economic status.
  • Empowering Women: By removing the pressure of dowry, we take a significant step toward gender equality, ensuring daughters are viewed as a blessing rather than a financial liability.

Through initiatives like these, Kalikamai Youth Society continues to transform lives, proving that with collective effort and a strong moral compass, we can eradicate detrimental practices and uplift the most vulnerable members of our society.
